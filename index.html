Arun Chavan | Evolutionary Developmental Biology

My name is Arun Rajendra Chavan. I am an evolutionary developmental biologist who studies how novel anatomical structures originate and diversify.

My research interests include origin of cell types, evolution of mammalian pregnancy, evolution of the immune system, and evolution of gene expression.

I received my Ph.D. in Ecology and Evolutionary Biology from Yale University in 2018. There, I was advised by Dr. Gunter Wagner, and I studied the origin of a novel cell type in the placental mammal lineage — the decidual stromal cells of the uterus.

Currently, I am a CRI Irvington Postdoctoral Fellow in Dr. Ruslan Medzhitov's lab in the Department of Immunobiology at Yale University, School of Medicine. The focus of my work here is the origin of immune cell types in vertebrates.
